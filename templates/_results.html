How to read this
Plain English — what each panel below actually means

The big colored header at the top is the verdict — 🔴 red / 🟡 amber / 🟢 green rolled up from the seven signals below by a transparent rule (no black-box model). Red ≥ 8, amber 3–7, green < 3.

The seven panels below are the evidence the verdict was built from. Each pulls from a public data source and cites the standard it screens against (ICVCM CCP #5, UNDRIP, ILO 169, Sabin Center, World Bank, TI CPI).

  1. Indigenous-territory overlap — does the project sit on Indigenous land? If yes, the FPIC obligation applies and the analyst pulls consultation records.
  2. Adverse media — has any English-language outlet flagged this project? Article scores as adverse only when a safeguards topic AND an adverse-claim term co-occur (kills "Project secures FPIC" false positives).
  3. Active litigation — is there an open court case naming this project or developer? Sabin Center registry + news inference.
  4. NGO complaints — have CMW, Survival International, Forest Peoples, or Mongabay reported issues?
  5. Environmental band — country-level forest-cover trend (World Bank). RED ≤ −0.3 pp/yr per FAO threshold.
  6. Governance band — TI CPI 2024, anchored to TI's published global average of 43.
  7. FPIC procedural checklist — 4 procedural checks anchored to UN-REDD operational FPIC guidelines: consultation · consent · grievance mechanism · withdrawal protections.

At the bottom, an LLM writes the Safeguards section of an IC memo from the cited evidence. A grounding sniff flags any term in the output that didn't appear in the evidence. The analyst still makes the call — Sentinel is the screen, not the verdict.
